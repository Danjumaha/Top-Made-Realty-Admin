Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services

Buying Properties

What documents do I need to buy a property in Nigeria?

You'll need a valid ID (International Passport, National ID, or Driver's License), proof of address, bank statements (6 months), and proof of income. For foreigners, you'll also need a residence permit. We'll guide you through the entire documentation process.

How long does the property buying process take?

Typically, the process takes 4-8 weeks from offer acceptance to completion. This includes property inspection, legal checks, payment processing, and transfer of title. Cash purchases are faster, while mortgage approvals may take longer.

Can foreigners buy property in Nigeria?

Yes, foreigners can buy property in Nigeria. However, there are some restrictions on land ownership in certain areas. Foreigners can acquire leasehold interests for up to 99 years. We specialize in helping international clients navigate these requirements.

What are the additional costs when buying a property?

Beyond the purchase price, budget for: legal fees (5-10% of property value), stamp duty (0.5-1.5%), registration fees, survey fees, and agent commission (typically 5%). We provide a detailed breakdown of all costs before you make an offer.

Renting Properties

What is the typical rent payment structure?

In Nigeria, rent is typically paid annually or bi-annually in advance. Some landlords may accept quarterly payments, especially for commercial properties. Monthly payments are rare but can be negotiated for certain high-end properties.

What's included in the rent?

This varies by property. Some rentals include utilities (water, electricity), security, and maintenance. Others are exclusive of these costs. Always clarify what's included before signing the lease agreement. We ensure all terms are clearly documented.

Can I negotiate the rent price?

Yes, rent is often negotiable, especially for long-term leases or if you're paying multiple years upfront. Market conditions, property demand, and your negotiation skills all play a role. We can help you negotiate the best possible terms.

Selling & Leasing

How much is your commission for selling properties?

Our standard commission is 5% of the final sale price for residential properties and 3-5% for commercial properties. This includes marketing, viewings, negotiations, and transaction management. We offer competitive rates and exceptional service.

How long will it take to sell my property?

The average time to sell varies by location, price, and market conditions. In prime areas like Victoria Island and Lekki, properties typically sell within 2-4 months. We use aggressive marketing strategies to minimize time on market.

What documents do I need to sell my property?

You'll need: Certificate of Occupancy (C of O) or Deed of Assignment, approved building plan, tax clearance certificate, recent utility bills, and valid ID. If the property is mortgaged, you'll need a letter from your bank. We'll verify all documents before listing.

General Questions

Do you offer property management services?

Yes, we offer comprehensive property management services including tenant screening, rent collection, maintenance, repairs, and financial reporting. Our management fee is typically 10% of monthly rent. We handle everything so you don't have to.

How do I schedule a property viewing?

You can schedule viewings through our website, by calling us at 08036909700, or via WhatsApp. We offer flexible viewing times including weekends and evenings. For international clients, we also provide virtual video tours.

Is my information kept confidential?

Absolutely. We maintain strict confidentiality for all client information. Your personal and financial details are protected under our privacy policy and Nigerian data protection regulations. We never share your information without your consent.
